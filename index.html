My Diabetes Kit

CLIENT Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine
SKILLS Product Management
Manufacturing
TIMELINE June 2022 — Present

Problem

Type II Diabetes is a chronic disease in which a person’s body produces insufficient insulin, a hormone necessary for glucose uptake in cells. Improper treatment of diabetes can seriously damage the nervous and cardiac systems.

Treatment involves many significant lifestyle changes. For example, many people with diabetes must check their glucose levels and inject insulin three or four times per day for the rest of their lives. Currently, there is insufficient support for teaching new diabetes patients how to monitor and manage their own health.

People are often hospitalized soon after their initial diagnosis because they aren't prepared or taught how to properly manage their own diabetes treatment.

Solution

Our team of doctors, researchers, and engineers designed and manufactured a do-it-yourself diabetes management kit that:

  • simplifies the treatment process into four straightforward steps (preparation, glucose checking, insulin injection, and cleanup)
  • includes all the supplies needed for diabetes treatment (glucose meters, insulin pens, syringes, etc.)
  • provides clear physical and digital copies of their treatment plan to walk the patient through the process
  • packages everything in a well-organized and compact kit that can be easily used by anyone

My Role

As a product manager, I streamlined communication between the Segal and Feinberg sectors of the My Diabetes Kit project by sending clear, detailed updates about manufacturing progress, quality control, and design updates at least once a week.

I created a straightforward system for tracking and ensuring that all kits were deemed high-quality and patient-ready by two separate quality control teams. Each kit was carefully assessed on five key aspects (quality of box, wrapping, insert, label, strap).

When unforeseen quality issues arose, I designed and implemented solutions rapidly to minimize delays in production. I also organized manufacturing reports and documentation on these solutions to ensure that project leads were informed and supportive of design changes.

I also organized a manufacturing team of eight students to create a total of 120 diabetes management kits for a clinical trial.

Each kit consisted of four magnetic boxes held together with a velcro strap. Each box contained custom-designed, user-friendly inserts to fit diabetes treatment supplies neatly and securely.

Manufacturing involved 3D printing, laser cutting, securing, and vacuum forming parts using tooling that we designed specifically for the low volume production process.

SteadyScrib

CLIENT N/A
SKILLS Engineering Design
Interaction Design
TIMELINE Oct. 2022 — Present

PLEASE NOTE: This project remains under NDA and certain details cannot be disclosed.

Problem

Parkinson’s disease is a long-term neurodegenerative disorder where a person’s motor system progressively degrades over time. Symptoms include tremors, stiffness, balance and coordination difficulties, etc.

While the cause remains unknown and no cure exists, the disorder involves cell death in a region of the brain called the substantia nigra, which is essential for motor coordination.

People with Parkinson’s disease are losing the ability to write because of hand tremors, bradykinesia (slow or inhibited movement), and rigidity.

Solution

The SteadyScrib team designed and manufactured a pen for people with Parkinson’s disease that:

  • uses a magnetic clipboard and pen to reduce hand tremors during writing
  • has a weighted pen core to counter bradykinesia and facilitate intentional movements
  • accommodates hand rigidity with a wide, pliable, and ergonomic grip

My Role

I collaborated with other engineers and designers to research, design, and prototype the SteadyScrib pen and clipboard to improve writing ability for people with Parkinson’s disease. I'm also working on UX and packaging design with the Steadyscrib marketing and communications team.

As a team, we have conducted literature reviews of research on Parkinson’s, sketched and researched possible solutions, created functional products with CAD modeling and rapid prototyping, worked with patients from the Parkinson's Foundation and Northwestern Medicine to test and improve our prototypes, as well as researched materials and manufacturing methods.

I love working with others on user research and prototyping for a product that has such immediate impact on people’s lives.

NURJ Design
Workshops

CLIENT N/A
SKILLS Service Design
Visual Design
TIMELINE Sept. 2019 — Present

Problem

The Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal (NURJ) is an annually published student and faculty peer-reviewed research journal. It showcases a selection of senior theses from graduating Northwestern students and interviews with faculty and student researchers on the research process.

Despite the good editorial content, NURJ designers were struggling with producing a professional, visually consistent journal due to a lack of experience with design principles, software, and teamwork.

Solution

The NURJ design team knew for a long time that there was insufficient training and support for new NURJ designers, many of whom had never done graphic design before.

Our solution was to run two graphic design training workshops that would:

  • outline and explain our design process for individual and collaborative projects in NURJ
  • help new designers understand what NURJ is and how their design work contributes to the larger organization
  • ensure that our design process is easy to understand and accessible for anyone, even those completely new to graphic design

My Role

With input from my fellow designers, I outlined, organized and ran design training workshops for new NURJ designers for the last three years. Our goal was for NURJ design to be as straightforward and approachable as possible.

I interviewed other designers to parse common graphic design pitfalls and their strategies for learning to overcome those difficulties. I combined those insights and used it to create the framework of the workshop, which included slides, reference sheets for design terms, examples of past designs, and some practice materials.

In our second year of running the training workshops, we realized that new designers would benefit from more direct feedback on their work. As a result, we organized office hours, implemented a design peer review system, and created a mentorship program pairing new and experienced designers to help everyone learn.
